Exploring gender linguistic bias in the overview of Wikipedia biographies.

Did you know...

With more than 9 billion visits per month over the last year, English Wikipedia is one of the main sources of information, so its articles can shape and influence the way people think.

Is there a difference?

In an ideal world, people should be described neutrally, in an objective way and without stereotypes. However, historically women have been described mainly by their physical appearance and have been usually related to their family role, whereas men have been related to their careers. Is this thought present in Wikipedia’s Biographies? If it is, to what extent?

The underrepresentation of women in Wikipedia

Did you know that only 17% of the more than 1.4 million biographies in Wikipedia belong to women? Surprised? So were we! Not only that, on average, men’s overviews are longer than women’s, 10% longer to be exact.

Most biographies belong to recognized people in their fields. There is a huge variety of occupations, ranging from very general descriptions like ‘writer’ or ‘athlete’ to more detailed ones such as ‘midfielder’ for footballers or ‘song writer’. All these occupations can be grouped into 10 different fields of occupations.

Athletes, Artists and Politicians

Sports, artists and politicians represent the 73% of all the occupation fields in the biographies .

One woman, more that 3 men

In almost all the 10 fields, men represent a majority. The only exception is the modeling industry, in which women predominate.

Insights on occupations in Wikipedia:

Model

Only one field of occupation has a higher female presence than males: models. This field represents those occupations related to the mode industry, mainly being models.

Military and Religion

The fields with higher disparity are Military and Religion, two fields of occupation where powerful positions have been historically reserved to men. If you think about it, this result may be deceiving but not surprising at all. Let’s do the test: in five seconds, how many military names can you come up with?[...] How many of them are women? Artist

Among the most represented fields, Artist is the one with more parity with a ratio of 2.7 men for each woman represented.

So far, we have seen that Wikipedia is different in terms of numbers for men and women, but is this platform different in terms of contents as well?

Gender stereotypes are still here

An in-depth analysis showed us that people writing on Wikipedia do not use neutral manners but biased ones to portray men and women. For example, think of the famous couple: Michael Douglas and Catherine Zeta-Jones, both have astonishing careers. They have been married for a while now and have 2 children together.

If you look at the overview sections on their Wikipedia biographies, you can spot some differences in terms of the words used to describe them.

First, we extracted the different adjectives from the biographies and ranked them based on their subjectivity and strength.

The 2 genders have similar results. In both cases, the presence of weakly subjective adjectives is higher than the one for strong ones. Another fact is that the usage of adjectives with positive connotations is preferred in front of neutral or negative ones. On the plot, we can see that for both genders, the distribution is almost the same..

These initial results made us think there might not be a difference in the way people depict both genders on Wikipedia. But of course, things are not always what they seem to be at first sight! A more in-depth analysis showed us that people writing on Wikipedia do not use neutral manners but biased ones to portrait men and women. Such analysis consisted of using a machine learning model that detected what we call as Linguistic bias, which definition is displayed below.

Linguistic bias is defined as a systematic asymmetry in word choice that reflects the social-category cognitions that are applied to the described group or individual(s). This translates for us in a way that, if there exists such a bias, our model will be able to detect patterns in the given adjective and decided the gender of the person being described. If such a bias does not exist, the model will perceive them equally and therefore, won’t be able to make a prediction.

When we observe these two overviews, we can spot some differences in terms of the words used to describe them. Roles that highlighted her sex appeal, her struggle with depression, and the fact that she is married to Micheal Douglas and has two children with him are the main things one can find in Zeta-Johns' overview. Whereas for Micheal Douglas, the reader finds mainly acting accomplishments and a tiny sentence referring to his marriage by the end of his overview, nothing about his children though.

But, what if we took a deeper look?

In order to analyse if there exists a difference between the descriptions based on the gender, we have developed a model using machine learning techniques. This model receives as input a series of words appearing in the overview we want to analyse. Its output is the probability that the overview belongs to a woman.

The idea behind this model is that if both genders are equally described, the model won't be able to identify the corresponding gender. In case it is able to perform an assignment, it means there is something in the data that can be learnt in order to identifying the gender. In other words, it means there is a bias.

Exploring gender bias in the overview of Wikipedia biographies.

Discover More

Did you know...

Only 17% of the more than 1.3 million biographies in the English version of Wikipedia are related to women.

Surprised? Well, so were we

It is clear that women biographies are a minority in Wikipedia, but how are these biographies actually written, how do people describe men and women on this platform. On this web page, we try to answer the previous question by analyzing the overview section in each of the studied biographies.

Wikipedia Fact-check

First things first, let us start with a quick fact-check about Wikipedia’s biographies. On average, men's biographies have a longer overview section than women's overviews, 10% longer to be exact.

One thing you may think at this point is, "okey but not everyone appears in Wikipedia". This is actually true. Although it is possible to create your own Wikipedia page, most biographies belong to people recognized in their field. So one detail we decided to explore further was the occupation of the different personalities.

There is a huge variety of occupations, ranging from very general descriptions like “writer” or “athlete” to more detailed ones such as “midfielder” for footballers or “songwriter”. In order to obtain some informative results, we grouped the different occupations into 10 fields.

Field of Occupation distribution in Wikipedia biographies

The field most represented among the different biographies is Sports followed by Artists and Politics. The following question came up straight forward: what’s the ratio of man:woman along with the different occupations?

Field of Occupation by Gender in Wikipedia biographies

In almost all the 10 fields, mean represent a majority, more than 2 men per every woman. The only exception was the Modeling industry, in which women predominate.

A Linguistic Tour for Data Science.

According to Wikipedia, linguistics is the scientific study of language. It involves analyzing language form and its meaning in context[1]. The previous is really important as we would like to understand the linguistics people use to portrait mean and women.

We began by studying the adjectives used in the overviews. Why not all the words? Adjectives are subject to higher subjectivity. It is not the same to say “he is a good actor” than “he is not a bad actor”. In other words, they allow the introduction of some more subjective perspective in what should be a completely objective description.

First, we extracted the different adjectives from the biographies and ranked them based on their subjectivity and strength.

The results this time were more similar. In both cases, the presence of weakly subjective adjectives is higher than the stronger ones. Also, the usage of adjectives with positive connotation is preferred in front of neutral or negative ones. But the main point to highlight is that for both genders, the distribution is the same.

These results gave us some hope. Maybe we were not perceived and described that different and there was no bias to be found. But of course, things are not always what they seem to be at first sight!

Linguistic bias is defined as a systematic asymmetry in word choice that reflects the social-category cognitions that are applied to the described group or individual(s). This translates for us in a way that, if there exists such a bias, our model will be able to detect patterns in the given adjective and decided the gender of the person being described. If such a bias does not exist, the model will perceive them equally and therefore, won’t be able to make a prediction.

When we observe these two overviews, we cannot spot many difference in terms of the words used to describe them. In both cases, they are described as 'professional tennis player', it is an emphasis on their awards and performance along their careers and some insight about other personal details.

But, what if we took a deeper look?

In order to analyse if there exists a difference between the descriptions based on the gender, we have developed a model using machine learning techniques. This model receives as input a series of words appearing in the overview we want to analyse. Its output is the probability that the overview belongs to a woman.

The idea behind this model is that if both genders are equally described, the model won't be able to identify the corresponding gender. In case it is able to perform an assignment, it means there is something in the data that can be learnt in order to identifying the gender. In other words, it means there is a bias.
